Typography is a key factor to user interface in websites. It sets the tone of your website and not only that, but it can help the viewer understand more about what your website is bringing to them. There are more factors to what comes into choosing your fonts, depending on what the purpose of your website is. In “Why typography is so important to your web design” by AWEBCO  it shows why choosing a typeface is important and some tips that help. If your website is more serious that should guide you to what types of fonts you are leaning towards. While picking and choosing a font is important, it is also important for the user to be able to understand and read comfortably without any trouble. This is where readability comes to play. Readability helps the user read your website easily without any trouble such as the font being too small or too hard to distinguish. In another article “Designing for Readability – A Guide to Web Typography (with Infographic)” by Jordan DeVos she offers a guide to making your website more readable for the user and other practices. Another factor in successful typography is loading your fonts. In the article titled “Best practices for fonts” by Katie Hempenius, she goes into detail about the performances of fonts and how to make them better to work within your website. In typography for websites, it is important to take note that when loading fonts, it is essential to use a specific declaration in CSS, making your website readable, and the importance of choosing the right fonts.

            When choosing the right font, for your website it is important to ask yourself the purpose of your website. A good practice would be to have what kind of emotion you want the user to have with your website. In the article In “Why typography is so important to your web design” by AWEBCO , it explains how choosing a font for a website is important. “The reason that typeface and font choice is so important for your brand or company is that you want your fonts to evoke positive emotions and provide easy readability at the same time.”  This demonstrates that in typography the main aspect of typography and purpose is so that the user can have a good overall experience when navigating through your website. With easy readability, and the type of font you choose this will establish a good relationship between the user and the website. When this happens, it makes the user happy and more than likely they will visit your website again since it is easy on the eyes and readable. The importance of choosing the right font helps establish this important connection that web developers strive for with their websites.

            Making use of your typography by making it readable is a good use of making the user experience more successful. Since we already know the importance of choosing the right fonts and it uses. Let’s dive into a key practice to make sure your typography is readable. Something that I thought was important to do is what they mention in the article “Designing for Readability – A Guide to Web Typography (with Infographic)”. DeVos mentions “Test typography on screens and display settings that fall on the far edges of average—such as nearly-outdated smartphones, the largest hi-res monitors, and screens turned up blindingly bright.” This shows that testing typography on different screens will let you know in a better way how typography is affected on different devices. A common practice to test is to go on the inspect feature and change the different screen sizes. Doing this will ensure that your font will work on different sizes and that the readability stays constant no matter what device screen size they have. With testing you can test as you go and change and fix the font sizes or decide better options for your website. Although testing for readability on different screen sizes is important it is also important to choose a header font that is contrasting from your body text font. A lot of times when choosing a font we can make the mistake of choosing two fonts that are too contrasting and choosing a font that is made for it to be a header then a body text. In the example below you can see how having a neutral balance is important when making fonts readable for the user.

            While choosing fonts and the importance of readability comes into play it is important for the typography to load fast enough on the page. In essential specific declaration in CSS the  @font-face rile  is what they mention in the article “Best practices for fonts” by Katie Hempenius. She states, “At a minimum, it declares the name that will be used to refer to the font and indicates the location of the corresponding font file.” This shows that the @font-face rule declares the location and the name of the font. It dives into the where the font is and brings it to where it is needed. This helps a lot with loading the fonts on your page faster. When this happens the user is more likely to have a satisfactory experience on your website.
